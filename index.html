Vex


We compete in 2 different Vex competitions every year, bringing our 2 robots with us. Vex 11040A utilizes a (insert after Rich builds it). However, Vex 11040B utilizes a outward moving tray to pick up mobile goals and a arm with a grabber at the end to control yellow cones. Both robots have been successful in previous competitions and we hope to continue our successes to states, nationals, or even the World Championships.

The robots are created by splitting into commitees for efficiency. One committee is to design and build 11040A. This is led by our Director of Engineering and Engineering Notebook, who are assisted by the members of their committee. The committee for 11040B is led by our President and Vice-President, who are also assisted by various members. Our final committee is programming. This is one committee programming both robots led by the Director of Programming. Using these three committees, we can finish the robot in time for competitions.

About

We are the official Herricks High School Robotics Team, based in Long Island, New York. We are one of the largest clubs in our school. We compete in the First Tech Challenge and Vex Challenges. We have meetings every Tuesday from 3-4:30 and are always accepting members.
